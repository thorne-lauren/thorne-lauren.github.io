A Reflection on Napoleon Dynamite

by Lauren Thorne

I was a freshman in high school when Napoleon Dynamite was first released in theaters. I was reluctant to see it because I was in somewhat of a “rebellious” phase and anything that was overly liked by the general public was something I wanted to shun, and plus, the trailers I kept seeing didn’t sell it very much either. I was late to the game when I finally saw it in theaters. I went with my brother and my dad and the whole movie was such a bizarre and delightful experience. I had never seen anything even remotely like it before. Each character was so strange and endearing. Suddenly all the Tina, tater tot, and Vote for Pedro things I’d been seeing and hearing were all making sense.

It has been 15 years since Napoleon Dynamite was in theaters, and it all started here in Preston, Idaho. Here in this small part of Southeastern Idaho a legend was made. I’ve been feeling nostalgic lately so I may go digging through my old DVDs to give it another watch. It’s been a long time. It can also be rented on multiple streaming platforms across the internet.
