Protect Your Skin

by Lauren Thorne

Being born a red-head with very fair and sensitive skin, sun protection is something I can’t afford to ever overlook. Most folks only pay attention to it during the summertime, but proper skincare and sun protection is important all year round. When it is cold, thankfully most of our skin is covered up and thus protected from the sun, however, damage can still be done to what skin is still exposed. Snow is surprisingly reflective of the sun’s UV rays so it can still cause damage even when it is cloudy.

Those who participate in winter sports have to be especially careful about sun-protection. The risk for sun damage increases with higher elevation. To combat this, sunscreen should be worn on any exposed skin, including lips! If you are going to be spending an extended amount of time outside, you’ll want to being your sunscreen with you so that you can reapply every 3-4 hours.

For our non-winter sports folks, it is still important to make sure that your skin is protected, but a moisturizer or foundation with a little SPF should do the trick for short amounts of time outside. Not only do UV rays damage your skin, but it makes skin age faster too!
