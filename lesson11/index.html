Keep Your Fur Babies Warm

by Lauren Thorne

Keeping warm is so important for everyone in the family as we are in the throws of these cold winter months. Sweaters indoors and outdoors become a fashionable and comfortable way to stay warm, but we shouldn’t forget our furry friends! A high collar or a turtle neck can make a big difference for our furry friends. If you have a cat, it is best to keep them indoors so that they don’t get lost or sick from being outside in the cold weather. For dogs, keep them on leashes and put some clothes on!

Most dogs are lost in the winter than any other season due to them losing their scent more easily in the cold. Make sure your animals are microchipping and wearing their ID tags in case they do end up getting lost.

Our doggies still need to go outside for walks and such, so you have to be smart about it when it gets so cold outside. If you bath your dog, be sure that they are completely dry before taking them out for a walk (this is good advice for us humans too. Frozen hair is never in style). After your walk be sure to wipe down your dog’s legs and paws to get rid of any harmful chemicals that are used to melt ice that they may have picked up.

Keep your furry friends warm!
